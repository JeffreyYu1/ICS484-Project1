London Cholera Outbreak 1854

The table above shows the dates with how many attacks on that date, how many deaths on that date, the total number of attacks up to that date, and the total number of deaths up to that date. To the right, is a line graph that represents the table above. The red line is # of Deaths per day, the blue line is # of attacks per day, the orange line is the total # of attacks up to date, and the yellow line is total # of deaths up to date. From these information, readers can see that during the end of August, there were a spike of attacks and deaths until a quarter into September.

The table above shows how deaths were related to each gender and is grouped by age ranges. For every death it equals to 10,000 deaths. To the right is a bar graph that represents the data in the table above. X axis represents the age groups and the y axis represents the # of deaths by 10,000. The more transparent bar represents the male gender and the less transparent bar represents the female gender. With this data, readers can see that the age group that got affected the most was over 80.

The table above shows the population of each gender in certain age groups. The Middle pie chart shows the percentages of the population to age groups for males. The most right pie chart is the same as the middle but its for the females. With these data, readers can conclude that there were much more younger people than older people.

The bar graph above shows the population of males compared to females and they are grouped by ages. The pie chart to the right is showing the same data but in a different chart. Readers can conclude from the data that there were more females than males when the age group is above 19.
